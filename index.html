PROJECTS

Function Generator

Designed using calculations by hand and simulated in NI multisim. Capabilities include waveform selection, output amplitude control, frequency range control, and frequency control. Was a great experience building the various stages. The design & problem solving aspect (i.e. being able to make design choices and developing solutions) was the most fun aspect.

Digital Dice

Programmed using assembly language. Push and hold button to cycle through numbers 1 to 9 extremely quickly (not visible to human eye). Then release button to stop at a number (creates effect of random number selection). Used 555 timer, AND gates, programmable chip and 7-segment display. First time playing around with EEPROM (i.e. programmable) chips and assembly code. Was a great learning experience

Radio

Features variable capacitor made using aluminum foil and paper for tuning audio quality. Coil with "taps" to select frequency. Most components were made from house hold items, Op-amp (LM741CN) was later used as diode and for amplification & improving signal quality even further.

Autonomous Robot

Made using arduino, ultrasonic sensors and infrared sensors (under robot). Made to fight other robots in a sumo-style battle. Detects surroundings using sensors and adapts accordingly. Robot body (purple) designed in CAD software and 3D-printed.

HoverCraft

Built during my first year at University Engineering Competition. All participants given same budget and task. Had 6 hours to design, build, test, and present solution to judges. Worked with a team of 4 to develop a solution in a high stress environment - given the limitations (e.g. time, budget, limited number of tests). Won 3rd place out of 100 fellow Engineering students

USB Warmer

First time doing crochet. This is great for keeping USBs warm during the winter. Can also double as a finger warmer (only one finger at a time though)

Mouse Holder

Playing around with crochet techniques - doing random stuff. Ended up with something that fit can fit a mouse (both kinds). Was a great learning experience - used some of these techniques to make the laptop case later.

Crochet Laptop Case

Laptop case made using crochet techniques. Took around 1 week to complete, requiring alot of focus and patience. Great for keeping laptop screen and keyboard clean as well!
